Python: Project #Repeal with Twitter and Sentiment Analysis

May 25th 2018 is likely to be date remembered in Irish history for years to come.
With such polarising and emotive debate on both sides I wanted to determine how the public feels about the Pro-Choice side on Twitter. To do that, I needed to use some kind of text analyzer for social media. I used TextBlob to effectively mine Twitter data for various opinions.

Textblob Sentiment Analysis

Textblob works on the basis that each word in the lexicon scores for polarity and subjectivity.
1. Polarity: negative vs. positive = (-1.0 => +1.0)
2. Subjectivity: objective vs. subjective = (+0.0 => +1.0)

The Scores

To begin I scraped 2100 tweets that contained any one of the following hashtags:

Keyword list= '#repealthe8th', '#together4yes', '#RepealThe8th', '#repeal8'.

The average polarity score was 0.08.
The average subjectivity score was 0.30.

The data highlights that tweets including Yes hashtags were more objective than subjective, indicating feeling was unbiased or based on fact. I was initially surprised when I calculated the polarity scores. When I looked further at the type of tweets and the language being used it became clear that this negativity was not towards the Yes side but towards the No side. Sentiment was particular negative towards No posters and Anti-Choice tweets. Although I didn't set out to analyse individual words what became apparent was that the Yes side

rarely mentioned the word abortion. In fact I took a look at the 100 most used words and the word abortion was not there. The words highlighted on the left represent the words used most frequently alongside Yes hashtags. I might be paraphrasing here but to me the most used words read as: "Together, we can vote to improve the health of Irish women."
