Butler for Trello

Trello is a great tool for managing projects and helping you stay on top of tasks but have you ever wished there was some kind of automation feature in the background that would act as a little helper, alleviating you from hundreds of tedious clicks? Well now there is and it’s called Butler for Trello. Simply put, Butler is a tool that lets you automate your Trello workflows. Key Benefit = IT IS A PRODUCTIVITY POWERHOUSE!

Butler Power-Up Vs ButlerBot

With Butler for Trello you have two options: The Butler Power-Up and the Butler Bot. With the Power-Up there are buttons created on the card or board itself. The functions behind these buttons need to be executed manually. The ButlerBot, on the other hand, is doing everything automatically whenever a trigger occurs. So for today, we are going to focus on the ButlerBot as this what you really want to master to save yourself time on a daily basis and increase all round efficiency. So that we know what we’re getting into here are just some of the tasks that Butler can do for you:

  • Creating new cards with descriptions in specified lists
  • Creating, archiving, and arranging lists
  • Moving and archiving cards in a list
  • Sorting cards in a list by date, name, votes, custom field, labels, and time on list

Getting Set Up

First things first. Let’s get Butler installed and invite the ButlerBot to a board of your choice. For ease of use you can use this link. Go ahead and accept the permissions and you’ll find yourself in the Butler Dashboard. This is where the magic happens.

The Butler Dashboard

Once you’re in the Butler Dashboard you should see a list of your boards. Choose the board you want to work out of by inviting Butler. This will essentially add Butler as a user on your board. Next we’ll give Butler some commands to take care of so we can get some of those tedious manual jobs off your plate and into Butler’s.

The Command Builder

The Command Builder is your new best friend! Let’s get started by building one, simple command. What I find super handy is creating one master list and adding cards from smaller, feeder lists into it. Here’s a scenario; Let’s say you’re the manager of a multi-functional team and you want oversight of all the activities and tasks your team members are carrying out. Create a master list, let’s call it Marketing Activities All. Imagine you have one team member updating a Blog articles list everytime a fresh article is published. So what we’re going to do is have your master list updated automatically every time that happens.

Next Steps
